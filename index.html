Short sories

My first great lesson : know how to question yourself

The story took place when I was sixteen or seventeen years old. I was trained to take care about children because I needed to work during holidays. At the end of the first week, the woman who taught us wanted having an interv\ iew with each of us to debrief about the training.
Then, she received me and she blamed me about my behavior. I mishandled what she said. So, she explained me the first professional skill is the ability to question oneself. I didn’t \ get it immediately, but since this moment I really try to question myself every day in my professional life of course but also in my personal life too.

Organization and collaboration

During my experiences in an accounting firm, I had to follow strict rules regarding confidentiality of course, but also and above all regarding security, business continuity and collaboration. Thus, it was essential to plan a scenario daily for different extreme situations.
- My computer no longer turns on or when I arrive at the office, the building is destroyed (yes, really!). How to recover the data and how to\ be able to work as quickly as possible?
- My car has broken down and I can't get to work. What solutions?
- When I leave the office, have I finished my work in a way that would allow my colleagues to resume it peacefully if I wer\ e to die that night?

with the right tools, stress becomes positive

After 10 years of experience in business relations, I experienced a lot of stress and received a lot of pressure. This experience allowed me to develop time management and organizational techniques in order to reduce stress a\ s much as possible. The reasoning is simple: if I have time management tools and enough autonomy to organize my priorities, stress becomes a driving force. Otherwise, it is essential to communicate with your managers to avoid being over\ whelmed and having no control over the various pressures.

And today

All my life, I have always had ambitions and goals that were very difficult to achieve because I chose them based on my weaknesses and not my strengths. After 40, I finally chose to do a job that suits me and in which I enjoy working. The hours of coding follow one another like hours of leisure. I found my new puzzle and the possibilities are endless when viewed from the edge \ of the ocean that is IT development.
